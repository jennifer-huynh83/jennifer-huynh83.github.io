Bridging the gap between software development and user experience

Hello, I'm Jennifer Huynh

I am a senior at the University of Washington Tacoma, graduating in Spring 2025 with a major in Computer Science and Systems and a minor in Innovation and Design. I focus on full-stack development with a strong emphasis on user experience, and I’m passionate about building thoughtful, user-centered technology that makes a real impact.

I’ve collaborated in fast-paced, cross-functional (multidisciplinary) teams across both web development and user design, and recently joined a startup team that’s driven by user insights in the product development process. I thrive in collaborative environments and love working with tight-knit teams dedicated to solving meaningful problems.

I'm currently seeking opportunities where I can continue growing as a developer while contributing to projects that prioritize accessibility, impact, and innovation.

A Little More About Me

I grew up in the South Seattle area, where I spent most of my time in school, playing sports, or participating in after-school programs. I later attended the University of Washington in Seattle for a little over a year, where I discovered my interest in web development. Wanting a more close-knit environment to grow in, I transferred to the University of Washington Tacoma to continue pursuing my passion.

After transferring, I discovered the User-Centered Design minor. It felt like fate—an opportunity to follow a creative path within tech while also focusing on helping people. The combination of design and technology opened up a meaningful direction for me, and I’ve been exploring it ever since.
